Recipe to make great Arepas

Description

Colombian Arepas are a beloved traditional dish, simple yet versatile, deeply rooted in Colombia's culinary culture. Colombian arepas are known for their versatility. They can be enjoyed plain or split open and stuffed with a variety of fillings like cheese, ham, or scrambled eggs. In some regions, they're also topped with butter or served with accompaniments like avocado or hogao, a traditional Colombian sauce made from tomatoes, onions, garlic, and cilantro. The result is a delightful combination of crunchy exterior and soft, warm interior, making arepas a comfort food enjoyed at any time of the day, from breakfast to dinner, reflecting the rich and diverse culinary traditions of Colombia.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of pre-cooked white cornmeal (masarepa)
  • 2 to 2 1/2 cups of warm water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • Optional: grated cheese, butter for serving

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix the pre-cooked cornmeal (masarepa) and salt.
    • Gradually add the warm water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Start with 2 cups of water, adding more if the dough feels too dry.
    • Knead the dough for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and pliable. It should not stick to your hands. If it's too dry, add a little more water; if too wet, add a bit more cornmeal.
  2. Shape the Arepas:
    • Divide the dough into equal portions, rolling each into a ball. The size of the balls can vary depending on your preferred arepa size.
    • Flatten each ball between your palms or against a flat surface to form a disc about 1/2-inch thick.
  3. Cook the Arepas:
    • Preheat a griddle or a non-stick pan over medium heat.
    • Place the arepas on the griddle and cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they have a nice golden crust and sound hollow when tapped.
    • Flip them occasionally for even cooking.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve the Arepas warm.
    • They can be split open and stuffed with your choice of fillings such as cheese, ham, or scrambled eggs.
    • Alternatively, serve them plain, with butter, or with traditional accompaniments like avocado or hogao.
